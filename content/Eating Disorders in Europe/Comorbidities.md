
Hambleton, A., Pepin, G., Le, A., Maloney, D., Aouad, P., Barakat, S., Boakes, R., Brennan, L., Bryant, E., Byrne, S., Caldwell, B., Calvert, S., Carroll, B., Castle, D., Caterson, I., Chelius, B., Chiem, L., Clarke, S., Conti, J., & Crouch, L. (2022). Psychiatric and medical comorbidities of eating disorders: findings from a rapid review of the literature. Journal of Eating Disorders, 10(1). https://doi.org/10.1186/s40337-022-00654-2
- for EDs in general, the most prevalent psychiatric comorbidities were anxiety (up to 62%), mood (up to 54%) and substance use and post-traumatic stress disorders (similar comorbidity rates up to 27%)
- associations between specific EDs and non-suicidal self-injury, personality disorders, and neurodevelopmental disorders
- medical comorbidities across the neuroendocrine, skeletal, nutritional, gastrointestinal, dental, and reproductive systems (these either precede, occur alongside EDs, or happen as a consequence of EDs)
- Bipolar disorder: BED (12.5%), BN (7.4%), AN (3.8%)
- MDD: AN (51.5%) and BN (54%)
- prevalence rates of OCD seemed to be highest among people with AN (lifetime = 19% and current = 14%) compared to other ED subtypes
- comorbidity between specific EDs and personality disorders is associated with common traits: constriction/perfectionism and rigidity are present in both AN and OCD (which had a heightened association), as was the case with impulsivity, a characteristic of both BN and BPD
- ADHD mostly with BN
- ASD and ARFID
- PTSD: between 16.1–22.7% for AN, 32.4–66.2% for BN and 24.02–31.6% for BED
- AN higher than BN for death by suicide, but BN higher than AN for risk of suicide
- non-suicidal self-injury: EDNOS (49%), BN (41%) and AN (26%)
