
Europe in general:

Pastore, M., Indrio, F., Bali, D., Vural, M., Giardino, I., & Pettoello-Mantovani, M. (2023). Alarming
Increase of Eating Disorders in Children and Adolescents. The Journal of Pediatrics,
263(113733), 113733–113733. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpeds.2023.113733
- general on Europe, mentions differences between countries
- a rise of around 40% in eating disorder cases in children and adolescents across several European countries since the start of the COVID-19 pandemic.
- In the UK, hospital admissions for young men grew by 128% between 2015 and 2021, while France and Italy reported doubling case numbers among students and emergency visits (Pastore et al., 2023).
- UK: the number of children and adolescents admitted to hospital with eating disorders has increased by 35% in 2022
- France: incidence of EDs in students: from 24.0% observed in 2009 to 46.6% in 2021 (which even 24% is a lot)
- prevention

Denmark:

Micali, N., Davies, H. L., Graff, H. J., Mortensen, L. H., & Hegelund, E. R. (2026). Temporal Trends in the Epidemiology of Eating Disorders Between 2000 and 2022: A Danish Register Study of Their Incidence and Comorbidities. European Eating Disorders Review, 34(3), 749-769. https://doi.org/10.1002/erv.70061
- new diagnoses of EDs have increased in Denmark over the last 22 years
- a decrease in the incidence of BN
- an increase in new OED diagnoses and an increase in AN cases during the COVID-19 pandemic at the population level
- these increases are specific to girls/young women below the age of 20 years
